RN FINANCIAL RESARCH CENTRE

Who we are

The RN Financial Research Center was established in 2014 by Rafael Nicolas Fermin Cota and Baran Kayhan to encourage interdisciplinary research in computational finance, primarily from a quantitative or mathematical perspective.

What we do

The research activities of the RN Financial Research Center are directed toward the study of financial markets and asset prices, capital structure and infrastructure financing, and the linkages between financial economics and other fields, such as biology, operational research, mathematics, computer science, psychology and marketing research.

Admission

The RNFC research program is intense and extremely competitive. Students enrolled in the research program are bright, highly motivated and keenly interested in computational finance. Successful research candidates will have to meet the following admissions requirements.

  1. Admitted to one of the dual degree HBA programs such as mathematics, computer science, engineering or economics.
  2. Have taken, at a minimum, two full semesters of study in differential and integral calculus, the caliber of which is required of engineering, math or science majors as well as ordinary differential equations, linear algebra, and a calculus based probability course.
  3. Have strong academic performance in mathematics and probability coursework.
  4. Willingness to learn the following:
    • High-Performance and Parallel Computing: execution speed, memory and distributed processing.
    • Optimization: Large-scale mixed-variable non-convex optimization: combinatorial or numerical, linear or nonlinear, convex or non-convex.
    • Machine Learning: Supervised learning, unsupervised learning, reinforcement learning, and deep learning.
    • Programming Languages: C/C++, Java, R, and Python

Applicants who lack some of these skills may still be considered provided that they take steps to acquire the necessary skills before entering their senior year as set forth in a conditional admittance by the admissions committee. For general information about the program, email Nico at rafael_nicolas@rnfc.org.
