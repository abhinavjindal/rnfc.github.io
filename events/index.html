Sept. 10 2014 - Scientific computing and reproducible research at the Ivey School of Business

My name is Nico and I am a lecturer in the Management Science group at the Ivey Business School. For the past 3 years I have been teaching End User Modeling and Advanced End User Modeling in the HBA program. My work frequently entails teaching data mining with R and C++, piloting new machine learning methodologies, and finding new and innovative ways to teach HBA students how to become a data scientist.

The use of data and interactive data applications to inform decision making is becoming ubiquitous in many domains. At major investment banks, hedge funds and consulting firms, most clients and colleagues are unfamiliar with advanced big data analytics, but increasingly understand its importance. With the help of RStudio, Shiny, and OpenCPU, now you are all able to create rapid analytic prototypes that change the types of end-products you will offer, and the ways in which your clients interact internally and externally.

I want to invite all of you on Sept 10th 2014 at 5:00 p.m. room TBD to a short presentation on a recently released technology at Ivey that provide a flexible, easy-to-use open-source tool for students wishing to create interactive predictive data applications. We also plan to discuss the means by which this technology solve multiple problems you will face in consulting, corporate finance, trading, accounting, and in any other business analyst role. We will then demonstrate web platforms I have created at RN Financial Corporation, which go well-beyond most private equity firms, and are full-fledged applications for building cash flow models and to compare private fund performance measurements.
